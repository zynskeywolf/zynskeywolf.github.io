Lore

I got introduced to musicc production around 2013 when I did my first experiments in recording DJ mixes using Atomix VirtualDJ 7 (yikes) and eventually making my own musicc using LMMS 0.4.x (more yikes), initially on Windows XP, then 7, then later on Linux after realizing how much more advanced its audio ecosystem was. At this time, I wasn't even thinking of publishing my musicc in any form yet.

The part that is worth writing about starts with my first album, Dark Matters. Released under the name DJ Pixus in 2020, it was basically a one-hour mix of my least embarrassing early works. Although the full album is no longer available in any form, you may still find some pirated reuploads if you really want to suffer. It was mostly experimental house-like tracks.

Shortly after that, I quickly changed my artist name to Steve Pixus, because I wasn't actually DJing anymore. For like a year or so, I released several tracks under this name, some in the old experimental house style, some uplifting trance, and some other random things. It was about the time when I created the current musicc channel on YouTube and migrated all the musicc there (thus confidently zeroing out all the stats).

Then, after realizing that releasing ridiculously different tracks under the same name is confusing af, I eventually came up with the Trancwolve alias somewhere in 2021. The exact meaning of this name depends on your mindset, but it's somewhere between "to transform and evolve" and "a wolfy who makes trance UwU". Since then, I use Trancwolve as my primary alias for trance and similar things, and Steve Pixus for "the rest".

After almost a year of procastination, my second big release was the Tranc Musicc album (very creative title, I know) in 2022, which is a collection of trance tracks from the aforementioned era. (That is why the artist names on that disc are such a mess.) It's safe to say that Your enormous amount of love and support was my primary motivation to finally finish it. Thanks again, once more.

Currently, I'm slooowly working on some new tracks for a brand new album, planning a remaster of Tranc Musicc, and occasionally doing live DJ sets on YouTube/Twitch. I also occasionally make tutorials and low-effort videos about Linux audio stuff or random braindead ideas.
